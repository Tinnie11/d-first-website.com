Glossary of Medical Terms

  • Anatomy: The study of the structure of the human body

    Anatomy is an essential subject in nursing education as it provides nurses with a detailed understanding of the body's organs, tissues, and systems.

  • Physiology: The study of how the body functions

    Physiology explores the mechanisms by which living organisms, including humans, perform their vital functions. Understanding physiology is crucial for nurses to comprehend normal body processes and identify abnormalities.

  • Pathophysiology: The study of the changes in bodily functions associated with disease

    Pathophysiology examines the physiological processes occurring in the body as a result of disease or injury. Nurses use knowledge of pathophysiology to assess, diagnose, and treat patients effectively.

  • Pharmacology: The study of drugs and their effects on the body

    Pharmacology is a critical component of nursing education as it enables nurses to understand the actions, uses, and side effects of medications. Nurses apply pharmacological knowledge to administer drugs safely and monitor patients for adverse reactions.

  • Microbiology: The study of microorganisms

    Microbiology focuses on the identification, classification, and study of microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites. Nurses use microbiological knowledge to prevent the spread of infections and implement appropriate infection control measures.
